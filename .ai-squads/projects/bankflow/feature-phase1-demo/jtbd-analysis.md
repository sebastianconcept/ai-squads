# JTBD Analysis: Phase 1 Demo Flow

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive Jobs To Be Done analysis for the Phase 1 Demo Flow feature, identifying customer jobs, satisfaction gaps, and solution alignment for Brazilian accounting professionals.

## Customer Jobs Analysis

### Primary Job
**What job are customers trying to get done?**

Brazilian accounting professionals need to convert bank statements from various Brazilian banks into standardized formats that can be imported into their accounting software, enabling them to maintain accurate financial records and focus on higher-value advisory work.

### Job Context
**When and where does this job arise?**

- **Circumstance**: Monthly bank statement reconciliation, client onboarding, tax preparation, financial analysis
- **Context**: Office environment, typically during business hours, often under time pressure
- **Constraints**: Multiple bank formats, Portuguese language, Brazilian-specific terminology, tight deadlines

### Job Progress Steps
**How do customers currently progress through this job?**

1. **Receive Bank Statement**: Download or receive bank statement from client's bank
   - **Current Experience**: Manual download from bank portal, email receipt, or physical document
   - **Pain Points**: Multiple formats (CSV, PDF, Excel), different bank portals, inconsistent data structure
   - **Satisfaction Level**: Low - time-consuming and frustrating

2. **Analyze Statement Format**: Understand the structure and format of the bank statement
   - **Current Experience**: Manual inspection of columns, headers, and data formats
   - **Pain Points**: Each bank has different column names, date formats, and transaction categorization
   - **Satisfaction Level**: Very Low - requires expertise and time

3. **Convert to Standard Format**: Transform bank data into accounting software format
   - **Current Experience**: Manual Excel work, formula creation, data cleaning, format conversion
   - **Pain Points**: 2-4 hours per statement, error-prone, repetitive work, complex formulas
   - **Satisfaction Level**: Very Low - tedious and time-consuming

4. **Validate and Import**: Ensure data accuracy and import into accounting system
   - **Current Experience**: Manual verification, error checking, import testing
   - **Pain Points**: Data validation errors, import failures, reconciliation issues
   - **Satisfaction Level**: Low - error-prone and time-consuming

5. **Reconcile and Analyze**: Verify accuracy and perform financial analysis
   - **Current Experience**: Manual reconciliation with bank records, financial analysis
   - **Pain Points**: Time spent on data conversion reduces time for analysis
   - **Satisfaction Level**: Medium - this is the value-added work they prefer

## Job Satisfaction Gaps

### Identified Gaps
**Where are customers experiencing dissatisfaction?**

#### Time Consumption Gap
- **Current State**: 2-4 hours per bank statement for manual conversion
- **Desired State**: <30 minutes total processing time
- **Gap Size**: 75-85% time reduction needed
- **Impact**: High - prevents focus on advisory work

#### Accuracy Gap
- **Current State**: 5-10% error rate in manual conversion
- **Desired State**: <1% error rate
- **Gap Size**: 80-90% error reduction needed
- **Impact**: High - errors affect client trust and compliance

#### Format Complexity Gap
- **Current State**: Each bank requires different conversion approach
- **Desired State**: Universal solution for all Brazilian banks
- **Gap Size**: 100% standardization needed
- **Impact**: Medium - requires specialized knowledge

#### Repetitive Work Gap
- **Current State**: Same conversion process repeated monthly
- **Desired State**: Automated, one-click solution
- **Gap Size**: 90% automation needed
- **Impact**: High - affects job satisfaction and efficiency

### Gap Prioritization

3. **Repetitive Work** (Priority 3): Affects job satisfaction and efficiency
