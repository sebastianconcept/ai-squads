# JTBD Analysis: Pre-launch Page

## Customer Jobs Analysis

### Primary Job: "Be the first to know about innovative solutions"
**Functional Job**: Stay informed about new tools that can improve my work efficiency
**Emotional Job**: Feel ahead of the curve and innovative in my field
**Social Job**: Share valuable early access opportunities with colleagues

**Current Satisfaction**: Low - No systematic way to discover new tools early
**Desired Outcome**: High satisfaction with early access to valuable tools

### Secondary Job: "Evaluate new tools before committing"
**Functional Job**: Test and understand new tools before making purchasing decisions
**Emotional Job**: Feel confident in my tool choices
**Social Job**: Make recommendations based on thorough evaluation

**Current Satisfaction**: Medium - Can access demo but no early evaluation process
**Desired Outcome**: High satisfaction with comprehensive evaluation process

### Tertiary Job: "Stay competitive in my industry"
**Functional Job**: Access tools that give me competitive advantage
**Emotional Job**: Feel confident and capable in my professional role
**Social Job**: Be seen as an early adopter and innovator

**Current Satisfaction**: Medium - Limited access to cutting-edge tools
**Desired Outcome**: High satisfaction with competitive advantage

## Satisfaction Gaps Analysis

### High Impact, High Frequency Gaps
1. **Early Access Information**: Users want to know about new tools before general availability
   - **Impact**: High - Early access can provide competitive advantage
   - **Frequency**: High - Users regularly seek new tools
   - **Current Solution**: None - Users rely on word-of-mouth or random discovery

2. **Tool Evaluation Process**: Users need systematic way to evaluate new tools
   - **Impact**: High - Poor tool choices waste time and money
   - **Frequency**: Medium - Happens when evaluating new solutions
   - **Current Solution**: Limited - Only demo access after launch

### Medium Impact, High Frequency Gaps
1. **Professional Networking**: Users want to share valuable opportunities with colleagues
   - **Impact**: Medium - Builds professional relationships
   - **Frequency**: High - Regular professional interactions
   - **Current Solution**: Manual sharing of discovered tools

2. **Industry Awareness**: Users want to stay current with industry trends
   - **Impact**: Medium - Important for professional development
   - **Frequency**: High - Ongoing professional development need
   - **Current Solution**: Fragmented - Various sources for industry news

## Unintended Consequences Analysis

### Positive Unintended Consequences
1. **Brand Awareness**: Pre-launch page may increase overall brand recognition
2. **User Engagement**: Early signups may be more engaged users
3. **Feedback Collection**: Early users may provide valuable feedback
4. **Market Validation**: Lead capture validates market interest

### Negative Unintended Consequences
1. **Expectation Management**: Early signups may have unrealistic expectations
2. **Support Burden**: Early users may require more support
3. **Feature Creep**: Early feedback may lead to scope expansion
4. **Timeline Pressure**: Public pre-launch may create launch pressure

### Mitigation Strategies
1. **Clear Communication**: Set realistic expectations about launch timeline
2. **Support Planning**: Prepare support resources for early users
3. **Scope Management**: Maintain clear product vision despite feedback
4. **Flexible Timeline**: Build buffer time into launch planning
