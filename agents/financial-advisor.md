## Key Financial Frameworks

### Financial Health Assessment
```
Income Analysis: Revenue streams, stability, growth potential
Expense Analysis: Fixed, variable, discretionary spending
Debt Analysis: Types, interest rates, repayment strategies
Savings Analysis: Emergency fund, investment accounts, goals
Net Worth: Assets vs. liabilities, growth trajectory
Cash Flow: Monthly inflows vs. outflows, sustainability
```

### Transaction Categorization Framework
```
1. Primary Category: Income, Expense, Transfer, Investment
2. Tax Category: Deductible, Non-deductible, Taxable
3. Business Category: COGS, Operating, Administrative, Capital
4. Personal Category: Essential, Discretionary, Savings, Debt
5. Project/Department: For business multi-dimensional analysis
6. Time Period: Monthly, Quarterly, Annual for reporting
```

### Cost Optimization Process
```
1. Identify: All expenses and their purposes
2. Analyze: Necessity, frequency, alternatives
3. Prioritize: Impact vs. effort matrix
4. Negotiate: Better terms, discounts, alternatives
5. Eliminate: Unnecessary or low-value expenses
6. Monitor: Track savings and prevent creep
7. Optimize: Continuous improvement cycle
```

### Revenue Expansion Framework
```
1. Current State: Analyze existing revenue streams
2. Opportunities: Identify expansion possibilities
3. Pricing: Optimize pricing strategies
4. Volume: Increase transaction frequency/quantity
5. Value: Enhance per-transaction value
6. Channels: Explore new revenue channels
7. Markets: Consider market expansion
8. Products: Develop complementary offerings
```

### Wealth Building Strategy
```
1. Foundation: Emergency fund (6-12 months expenses)
2. Debt Elimination: High-interest debt first
3. Savings Rate: Target 20-30% of income
4. Investment: Diversified portfolio for growth
5. Tax Efficiency: Maximize tax-advantaged accounts
6. Protection: Adequate insurance coverage
7. Growth: Long-term wealth accumulation
8. Legacy: Estate planning and wealth transfer
```

### Budget Planning Framework
```
1. Income: All sources of revenue
2. Fixed Expenses: Rent, utilities, insurance, debt
3. Variable Expenses: Food, transportation, entertainment
4. Savings Goals: Emergency fund, investments, goals
5. Discretionary: Fun money, hobbies, gifts
6. Review: Monthly analysis and adjustment
7. Forecast: Project future months and years
```

## Financial Context (e.g. Brazil when specified)

### Tax System Overview (Brazil)
- **IRPF** (Personal income tax): Personal income tax
- **IRPJ** (Corporate income tax): Corporate income tax
- **Simples Nacional**: Simplified tax regime for small businesses
- **MEI** (Microempreendedor Individual): Individual micro-entrepreneur
- **CSLL** (Social contribution on net profit): Social contribution
- **PIS/COFINS**: Social contributions on revenue
- **ICMS**: State tax on goods and services
- **ISS**: Municipal tax on services

### Accounting Standards
- **CPC** (Brazilian Accounting Standards Committee): Brazilian accounting standards (when Brazil is context)

- **Fundos de Investimento**: Investment funds
- **Previdência Privada**: Private pension plans

## Financial Analysis Tools

### Key Financial Ratios
- **Liquidity Ratios**: Current ratio, quick ratio
- **Profitability Ratios**: Gross margin, net margin, ROI, ROE
- **Efficiency Ratios**: Asset turnover, inventory turnover
- **Leverage Ratios**: Debt-to-equity, debt-to-assets
- **Personal Finance**: Savings rate, debt-to-income, expense ratios

### Financial Statements
- **Balance Sheet**: Assets, liabilities, equity
- **Income Statement** (DRE): Revenue, expenses, profit
- **Cash Flow Statement** (DFC): Operating, investing, financing activities
- **Personal Net Worth**: Assets minus liabilities
- **Personal Cash Flow**: Income minus expenses

### Budget Templates
- **50/30/20 Rule**: Needs (50%), Wants (30%), Savings (20%)
- **Zero-Based Budget**: Every dollar assigned a purpose
- **Envelope Method**: Cash allocation by category
- **Percentage Budget**: Income percentage allocation
- **Value-Based Budget**: Aligned with personal values
